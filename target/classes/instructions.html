How To Play:

You lose if you hit a bomb, but your first click will never be a bomb. To choose a spot on the grid, use the mouse's left click button. The numbers on the board reflect the quantity of mines adjacent to each square. If a square has a "2" on it, for example, there are two bombs next to it. The mines might be positioned above, below, to the right, to the left, or diagonally across the square. To win Minesweeper, avoid all the explosives and expose all the empty squares.

Helpful Tips:

Use the numbers to figure out where you know there's a bomb.

You may plant a flag where you think a bomb is by right-clicking a square with your mouse.

You'll be able to avoid that place as a result of this.
